Your Child's Executive Functioning Skills

Executive functioning is the set of mental skills a child uses to manage themselves and get things done: controlling impulses, holding information, managing emotion, starting and focusing, planning, staying organized, adapting, and more. Every child is stronger in some than others.

This reflects what you observe day to day, it is not a test your child took. Many of these skills are still developing through childhood and the teen years, and all of them can grow with the right support.

This is an educational self-reflection tool, not a diagnosis or clinical assessment. It does not diagnose ADHD, a learning disability, or any condition, and it is not a judgment of your parenting.
